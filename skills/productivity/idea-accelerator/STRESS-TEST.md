# Stress-Test Rubric

Three kill questions for the top 2 variants from the idea-accelerator converge phase.

## The 3-question kill test

### Q1 — Failure mode
> "What would have to be true for this to completely fail?"

Look for: market timing, required preconditions (infra, regulation, behavior change), single points of failure, and dependencies on actors outside your control.

If the answer is "nothing plausible would cause it to fail" — the idea isn't specific enough yet. Sharpen it.

### Q2 — Competitive graveyard
> "Who already does something similar, and why haven't they succeeded?"

This is not a reason to kill the idea — it is a reason to understand what's different about *this* attempt. If you can't name a similar attempt, either you've found a genuine gap or you haven't done enough research. Name the closest analogue even if it's in a different industry.

### Q3 — Riskiest assumption
> "What is the riskiest assumption baked into this idea — and can you test it this week?"

Classify the assumption type:
- **User behavior**: "Users will actually do X"
- **Technical**: "This can be built at the performance level needed"
- **Market**: "This segment is large enough to matter"
- **Timing**: "This is the right moment for this to exist"

A good assumption test is: cheap, fast, doesn't require building the product, and produces a clear yes/no signal.

## Scoring calibration

| Total /15 | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 12–15 | Strong — pursue immediately |
| 9–11 | Promising — run one assumption test first |
| 6–8 | Risky — identify what would need to change |
| < 6 | Wild card — shelve but don't discard |
